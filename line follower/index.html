Line Follower Robot – Concept Overview

A Line Follower Robot works on the principle of detecting a path and adjusting its direction accordingly using IR sensors. It is one of the best beginner robotics projects. A line follower robot is an autonomous robot designed to follow a pre-defined path or line on the ground, typically a black line on a white surface or vice versa. It uses IR sensors to detect the line by sensing the reflection of light, and an Arduino UNO acts as the brain, processing these sensor signals to determine the robot’s movement. Based on the sensor input, the Arduino sends commands to a motor driver, which powers the motors to move the robot forward, turn left, or turn right, keeping it on the path. Line follower robots are widely used in educational projects, automated guided vehicles (AGVs), warehouses, and industrial applications.

How Infrared Sensors Work

IR sensors detect contrast between black and white surfaces. The black line absorbs infrared light while white reflects, helping the robot stay on track. Sensors (IR sensors): Detect the line on the surface by sensing light reflection (white) or absorption (black). They tell the robot where the line is. Arduino UNO: Acts as the brain. It reads the sensor signals and decides how the motors should move (forward, left, right) to follow the line.

Robot Assembly

The robot requires chassis mounting, motor installation, sensor placement, and Arduino programming. Each component must be aligned properly for smooth movement. Mount Components: Fix motors, wheels, Arduino UNO, and motor driver on the chassis. Place Sensors: Attach 3 IR sensors (left, center, right) at the front, near the ground. Wiring: Connect sensors to Arduino, Arduino to motor driver, motor driver to motors, battery to power everything. Program Arduino: Read sensor values and control motors to follow the line (left, right, forward). Test & Adjust: Place on line, test movement, fine-tune sensor position and code.
